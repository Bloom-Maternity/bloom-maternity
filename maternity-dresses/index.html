The Complete Guide to Maternity Dresses in Australia

Everything you need to know about finding maternity dresses that flatter, fit and feel incredible, no matter your trimester, budget or style.

Finding the right maternity dress shouldn't feel like a compromise. Whether you need something for work, a weekend brunch, a wedding or just because you want to feel good, the Australian maternity market has more options than ever before. This guide breaks down every style, fabric and fit so you can shop with confidence.

Maternity Dress Styles Worth Knowing

Wrap Maternity Dresses

Wrap dresses are a pregnancy staple for good reason. The adjustable tie means the dress grows with your bump, and the V-neckline is universally flattering. Look for wrap dresses in jersey or bamboo for maximum comfort, and choose a midi length for versatility. Many Australian brands, including Ripe Maternity and Bae the Label, offer excellent wrap dress options that work from the first trimester through to postpartum.

Ruched Maternity Dresses

Side ruching is one of the most flattering details on a maternity dress. The gathered fabric accommodates your growing bump while creating a streamlined silhouette. Ruched maternity dresses work well in stretch cotton or modal fabrics and are available as bodycon midis, fitted maxis and relaxed T-shirt dress shapes.

Maxi Maternity Dresses

A maternity maxi dress is a wardrobe workhorse. Comfortable enough for everyday wear, polished enough for dinners and events, and the long length means you don't need to worry about hemlines riding up as your bump grows. Knitted maxis in winter and lightweight cotton or linen maxis in summer will see you through the year in Australia.

Bamboo Maternity Dresses

Bamboo fabric has become hugely popular in Australian maternity wear, and for good reason. It's thermoregulating (keeping you cool in summer and warm in winter), incredibly soft against sensitive skin, and naturally moisture-wicking. Brands like Bae the Label and Soon Maternity have invested heavily in bamboo maternity dress ranges.

Nursing-Friendly Maternity Dresses

If you're thinking ahead to postpartum, nursing-friendly maternity dresses are a smart investment. These feature discreet feeding access, usually through a wrap front, button placket or layered panel. Buying dual-purpose dresses means you'll get more wear out of each piece and won't need to replace your entire wardrobe after birth.

How to Choose the Right Maternity Dress

Sizing is the biggest source of frustration when buying maternity clothes. Most Australian maternity brands recommend buying your pre-pregnancy size, as their designs already account for bump growth. However, if you're between sizes or expect significant body changes beyond your belly, sizing up is a safe bet.

Fabric matters more during pregnancy than at any other time. Your body temperature runs higher, your skin may be more sensitive, and comfort is non-negotiable. Prioritise natural fibres (cotton, bamboo, linen) and stretch blends (jersey, modal) over synthetic fabrics. These breathe better, wash well and feel better against the skin.

Think about what you'll actually wear the dress for. A bodycon ruched dress looks incredible for date nights but might not be what you reach for on a 35-degree Sydney day. Build a small collection of different styles rather than buying multiples of the same shape.

Best Australian Maternity Dress Brands

The Australian maternity clothing market is home to several standout brands. Ripe Maternity offers a broad range from casual to occasion wear. Bae the Label focuses on fashion-forward pieces that don't look like traditional maternity wear. Soon Maternity is known for quality basics and workwear. Pea in a Pod specialises in comfortable, well-priced everyday options. Queen Bee carries a curated mix of Australian and international labels.

For budget-friendly options, Cotton On Body and Target Australia both carry maternity dress ranges that punch above their price point. At the premium end, Apero Label and The Inarra offer designer-level maternity fashion.

Maternity Dresses by Trimester

First Trimester

Your bump is small or non-existent, but bloating and breast changes are real. Loose-fitting dresses, empire waists and stretchy fabrics work well. You may find you can still wear some of your regular dresses during this period, particularly shift dresses and A-line shapes.

Second Trimester

This is typically when your bump becomes visible and regular clothes stop fitting comfortably. Bodycon and ruched styles look fantastic as they show off the bump shape. Wrap dresses come into their own during the second trimester.

Third Trimester

Comfort is king. Look for dresses with maximum stretch, soft waistbands (or no waistband at all), and easy nursing access if you're planning ahead. Maxi dresses tend to be the most comfortable option in the third trimester as they don't constrict anywhere.
